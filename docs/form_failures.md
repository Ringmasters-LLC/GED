# Form Failures

## Hong Kong Postal Codes
HK does not use postal codes. Most US-based forms force a 5-digit number, causing users to enter "99999" or "00000", polluting the data.

## Japanese Address Order
Japanese addresses are written Country -> Postal Code -> Prefecture -> City -> ... 
Western forms reverse this, confusing local users and increasing entry error rates.

## Single Names in Indonesia
Many Indonesians have a single legal name. Forms requiring a "Last Name" force users to repeat their first name or use a dot, which is incorrect.

## UAE Address Layout
UAE often relies on PO Boxes or specific landmarks rather than street numbers. Standard "Street Address" fields are often insufficient or confusing.

## Ireland Eircodes
Ireland introduced Eircodes recently. Many forms still use old county-based validation that rejects valid Eircodes or fails to require them.
